# L-Carnitine Supplementation for Strength Athletes - What the Research Shows

## Outline

### Section 1: What is L-Carnitine and How Does It Work?
- Define L-carnitine (beta-hydroxy-gamma-trimethylammonium butyrate)
- Role in fatty acid transport into mitochondria
- Endogenous synthesis vs dietary sources
- Forms: L-carnitine, acetyl-L-carnitine, propionyl-L-carnitine

### Section 2: The Research on Strength and Power Output
- Meta-analysis findings on physical performance
- Studies showing improved peak and mean power output
- Dose-response relationships (2-4.5g range)
- Limitations: mostly cycling/sprint studies, limited resistance training data

### Section 3: Recovery and Muscle Damage
- Reduced blood lactate accumulation
- Improved post-exercise recovery
- Muscle injury reduction
- Anti-inflammatory effects

### Section 4: Practical Considerations for Lifters
- Optimal dosing (2-4g daily)
- Timing (with meals vs fasted)
- Form differences (L-carnitine vs acetyl-L-carnitine)
- Side effects and safety profile

### Section 5: Bottom Line - Is It Worth It?
- Who might benefit (endurance athletes, older lifters)
- Who probably doesn't need it
- Comparison to proven supplements (creatine, caffeine, protein)
