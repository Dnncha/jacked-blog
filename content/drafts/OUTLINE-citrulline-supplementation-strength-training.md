# Citrulline Supplementation for Strength Training: The Pump Amino Science

## Outline

### Section 1: What is Citrulline?
- Non-essential amino acid (L-citrulline vs citrulline malate)
- Found in watermelon (name from Latin for watermelon)
- Endogenously produced in urea cycle
- Different from arginine (which it converts to)

### Section 2: The Nitric Oxide Mechanism
- L-citrulline → L-arginine → nitric oxide synthase
- Why citrulline is more effective than arginine supplementation
- Blood flow, oxygen delivery, and "pump" effects
- Impact on vascular function and endurance

### Section 3: Research on Strength Performance
- Repetitions in reserve and volume load studies
- Time under tension and muscle endurance
- Strength-specific research findings
- Contrast with endurance-focused studies

### Section 4: The "Pump" - What the Research Shows
- Subjective reports vs objective measures
- Muscle fullness and cell volumization
- Training session quality and perceived exertion
- Practical implications for bodybuilders

### Section 5: Practical Considerations for Lifters
- Dosing: 6-8g L-citrulline or 8-10g citrulline malate
- Timing: 30-60 min pre-workout
- Stacking with other pump products
- Forms: Pure L-citrulline vs citrulline malate (2:1 ratio)

### Section 6: Bottom Line
- Who benefits: hypertrophy-focused lifters
- Not a replacement for proper training and nutrition
- Cost considerations
- Realistic expectations for strength vs pump
